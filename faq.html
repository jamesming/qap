Frequently Asked Questions

What is QAP?
The Queer Asylum Project is an online repository of stories in audio format—about people from around the world who have suffered persecution because of their sexual identity, and have sought or are seeking asylum in a country that accepts asylum applications on that basis.



What is QAP's Vision?
QAP's vision is that one day we will live in a world where every person is free to self-identify, and self-express their full humanity—including sexual identity—without fear.

What is QAP's Mission?
QAP provides a platform for lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgendered/ing individuals to share their stories of persecution so that others might be more informed of and sensitized to such injustices.

Why was QAP created?
The Queer Asylum Project was created by a handful of people who viscerally hold that increased awareness of injustice can lead to its eradication. QAP's founders took action after hearing the compelling story of one young man who suffered persecution because he is gay, and believed it needed to be told.

Who runs QAP?
The Queer Asylum Project is maintained by Richard Forhez and David Wishart, who are friends and colleagues.

What does QAP do?
The Queer Asylum Project provides and promotes a platform of audio histories of persecution of individuals who were mistreated because of their real or perceived sexual orientation.

Is QAP a non-profit?
Not yet, but we will be. We need funding to cover the costs associated with incorporating as a California non-profit, and then filing with the U.S. IRS for tax-exempt status as a 501 (c)(3). (Please help.)

Are my donations tax-deductible?
Not yet, but they will be. (See previous Q&A.) But if you feel this is a worthy cause, please give anyway.
