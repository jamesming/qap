In service of our mission, we strive to build and protect a reputation as a credible repository and publisher of true, accurate, and verifiable histories.



If you or someone you know has a story, we earnestly invite you to share it with the world through The Queer Asylum Project. (Stories can be of those living or dead.)

What we’re looking for…


  • Stories should be about people who have filed or are filing for asylum in a country that accepts such applications based on sexual orientation.

  • We like to see a copy of the person's official declaration (usually taken by an immigration attorney), and the official asylum application.

  • We may take steps to verify provided information, and we may opt not to publish a story if it's unverifiable.

  • We'll ask you make yourself available for an interview with an audio journalist to facilitate the capture of the audio content.

  • We really like photographs and/or video, if possible.

 


Process

  • Reach out to us using the contact form on the Contact page, and we'll get back to you in short order to discuss your story.

  • If all is good, we'll arrange for an interview with an audio journalist (and sometimes a separate recording engineer).

  • After post-production editing, we publish the story here.

  • We're more than happy to return all original photographs, and a copy of the unedited and published (edited) versions of the audio will also be available for you.
A platform for lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender individuals to share their stories of persecution so that others can be informed of and more sensitized to these injustices.
